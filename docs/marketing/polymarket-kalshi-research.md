## 0. VERIFIED — browsed directly (June 2026)

I opened the real sites and IG profiles instead of trusting summaries. First-hand findings:

Sports ("Game 4: San Antonio at New York" with Yes/No odds) and Politics markets up top, each with a tied news snippet.

**Polymarket Instagram (@polymarket):** **716K followers · 1,487 posts**, bio "Trade on politics, news, sports, culture & tech." Feed = **dark images with HUGE bold all-caps NEWS HEADLINES** ("ELON MUSK IS ON TRACK TO BECOME THE WORLD'S FIRST TRILLIONAIRE", "TRUMP WILL NOT BE ATTENDING GAME 4 OF THE NBA FINALS AT MSG"). **The gray Polymarket logo is watermarked top-left on every post** — "screenshot marketing" confirmed on real posts.

**Kalshi Instagram (@kalshi):** **424K followers · 1,696 posts**, bio "Trade on anything: halloween? politics, sports, entertainment, crypto, weather, and so much more." Same **news-jacking headline-card** style ("TRUMP SAYS HIS BIRTHDAY WISH IS WORLD PEACE", "NVIDIA IS NOW WORTH MORE THAN INDIA"), more photo-driven backgrounds, green branding.

**Verified takeaways that change the plan:**
- **Their #1 organic IG format is the bold all-caps NEWS-HEADLINE CARD, not the carousel.** They news-jack the day's biggest story into one punchy graphic. → Ledge should do this daily (it's cheap and on-brand) **and** own the carousel lane they underuse (educational/listicle).

- **Brand color = ownable.** Polymarket owns blue, Kalshi owns green. **Ledge should commit hard to its amber/streetwear palette** so its screenshots are instantly recognizable.
- **News + odds chart as content.** Polymarket literally puts breaking-news and a moving odds chart on the homepage. Ledge's animated odds + ticker (already built) are screenshot-ready content — post them.

## TL;DR — the 6 things that matter

1. **Kalshi grows with cheap, weird, AI-made UGC video** — a $2K AI ad (Veo 3) hit 3M+ views; their TikTok/IG/Reddit/YouTube ads star young people doing a "money hack."
2. **Polymarket grows with "screenshot marketing," founder hype, and news-jacking** — its logo rides on every screenshot; markets ARE the content.
3. **The shared formula: Money + Absurdity = Virality.** Both win by being culturally fast and meme-native, not polished.
4. **Ledge's unfair advantage: no real money = no gambling-ad rules.** Kalshi is getting flak for "money hack" ads aimed at under-21s. Ledge can run the *same* viral energy reframed around **clout and being right** ("I called it") instead of cash — legally clean, more shareable.
5. **Carousels in 2026 are won on slide 1 + completion rate.** 0.25s to stop the scroll; 7–10 slides; 80% completion → Explore page.
6. **What's viral now:** loud budgeting, "rich in life," before/after glow-ups, dramatic-audio overreactions, faceless screen-record channels, lo-fi AI video, 3-second payoff-first hooks.

## 1. POLYMARKET — the playbook

Polymarket treats **the market itself as the ad**. Five pillars (per HubSpot's teardown):

| Pillar | How it works | Steal this for Ledge |
|---|---|---|
| **Screenshot marketing** | A/B-tested a light-gray logo placement so it survives any crop. Every screenshot of odds on X/Reddit/TikTok carries the brand. | Bake a clean, crop-proof Ledge watermark + the odds bar into every shareable card. You already have share cards — make the logo un-croppable. |
| **Founder-led** | CEO Shayne Coplan tweets 100% Polymarket, 24/7; shares milestones (#1 App Store), explains mechanics, does podcasts (Nate Silver). | Founder/team account that posts only Ledge — wins, weird markets, milestones, "how odds work" explainers. |
| **Social proof** | Retweets anyone notable referencing them; posts screenshots of huge bets + news coverage. | Repost users who "called it"; screenshot wild leaderboard runs; celebrate every press mention. |
| **"The current thing"** | Spins up a market on whatever's trending within hours — including absurd ones (will a candidate say "skibidi"). | Your generator already does this. Turn the *funniest* fresh market into a daily post. Speed is the moat. |
| **Meme marketing** | In-house social team + freelance meme creators; branded merch on meme templates. Formula: **"Money + Absurdity = Virality."** | Same, minus money: **"Clout + Absurdity = Virality."** |

**Distribution unlock — the X partnership:** X named Polymarket its *official prediction market partner*; odds now annotate posts via Grok. They plugged into an audience 20,000× their size. Lesson: **partnerships/integrations > paid ads** for credibility and reach.

**Channels & tone:** X-first, business-but-playful, educational + irreverent. Instagram (@polymarket, ~717K) posts trading angles on politics/news/sports/culture/tech.

**Paid influence:** They've paid X political influencers (hundreds of K followers) thousands per post since 2024.

## 2. KALSHI — the playbook

Where Polymarket is organic/founder-led, **Kalshi floods paid social with cheap, weird, native-looking video.**

**The viral case study (the one to study):**
- AI-generated NBA Finals ad, **~$2,000, 2 days, Google Veo 3**, 3M+ views on X. Made by AI filmmaker **PJ Accetturo** (got his break via MrBeast DM).
- Creative direction: **"GTA VI meets Florida Man"** — rapid-fire, high-dopamine, lo-fi, "part meme, part digital fever dream."
- Process: generated **300–400 clips → curated the best 15.** Volume + curation, not polish.
- This is the visual language of 2025–26: **lo-fi AI chaos beats glossy production.**

**Their always-on UGC ads (TikTok/IG/Reddit/YouTube):**
- Star young people hyping the app. **Men → sports trading; women → "culture markets."** Targets ~under-21.
- Hooks are pure **"money hack / side hustle / FOMO":**
  - *"I literally made $761 and 7 cents last night… why aren't you guys doing this?"*
  - *"made $270 this week from my bed."*
  - *"how I just made $170 this week doing literally nothing but checking if it would rain."*
- Result: TikTok-style ads reportedly drove **$4.4B in volume**; prediction markets did **~$64B in 2025, +400% YoY.**

**⚠️ The opening for Ledge:** Kalshi is getting **publicly criticized** (Media Matters) for normalizing gambling to youth with misleading "easy money" claims. That's a legal/PR ceiling on their messaging. **Ledge has no real money → no gambling-ad rules → can out-meme them safely** by swapping "I made $761" for **"I CALLED IT and now I'll never let my group chat forget."**

## 5. CAROUSEL MASTERCLASS (your starting format)

**The 2026 carousel rules (data-backed):**
- **Slide 1 = 80% of the work.** Users scroll 3–4 posts/sec; you have **~0.25s** to stop them.
- **Size:** 1080×1350 (4:5 portrait) — max screen real estate.
- **Length:** **7–10 slides.** 10 maximizes dwell *if* completion stays >60%. Cut to 5–7 if it drags.
- **Algorithm signal (2026):** ranks on **active dwell time + swipe-through velocity**, not likes. Each swipe = an engagement event. **80% completion on a 10-slide deck → Explore page.**
- **Add "Swipe →" on slide 1:** only ~5% of carousels do; lifts engagement ~1.83%→2%, and +15–30% in other tests.
- **Consistency:** same type + color across slides (lower cognitive load).
- **Cover motion:** a subtle zoom/cinemagraph on slide 1 = +22% stop-power.
- **Final slide = one clear CTA.**

**Slide-1 design specs:** hook 5–8 words, 70–100pt, biggest thing on the slide; high-contrast (light-on-dark fits Ledge); keep text in the top 60% (IG UI covers the bottom).

**6 copyable hook templates (with Ledge fill-ins):**
| Type | Template | Ledge example |
|---|---|---|
| Question | "Are you [common action] wrong?" | "Are you betting the favorite *wrong*?" |
| Shock stat | "[X]% of [group] fail. Here's what the [Y]% do." | "92% of people fade the underdog. The 8% who don't are cleaning up." |
| Promise | "How I [result] without [obstacle]" | "How I went +4,000 credits this week without knowing sports" |
| List | "[N] [things] in [N] slides" | "6 markets closing tonight you'll regret skipping (swipe)" |
| Mistake/Myth | "Stop [mistake]. Do this instead →" | "Stop betting with your heart. Read the odds instead →" |
| Cliffhanger | "[Situation]… [vague payoff] →" | "Everyone said NO. The market just flipped. Wait til slide 6 →" |

## 6. LEDGE STRATEGY — how to apply all of this

**Your one-line positioning:** *"Polymarket's brain, TikTok's energy, zero of the money — pure bragging rights."*

**Content pillars (rotate these):**
1. **Today's wildest market** — screenshot the funniest fresh market. (Polymarket's "current thing.")
2. **"Called it" flexes** — user/leaderboard wins, payout reveals. (Social proof + glow-up.)
3. **Odds explainers** — "what 38% really means," teach the degen. (Founder-led education.)
4. **Reaction/meme** — dramatic-audio overreactions to a 3% odds swing. (Rides the trend.)
5. **Lists/"closing tonight"** — carousel of markets to drive opens. (FOMO + utility.)

**5 ready-to-build carousels (start here):**
1. **"6 markets closing tonight (you'll cope if you miss these)"** — cover hook + 5 market cards + CTA "Drop in before they lock → @ledge".
2. **"How to read the odds like a degen in 7 slides"** — educational, saves/shares well.
3. **"I called it. Receipts." ** — a win story, slide-by-slide payout reveal (before/after glow-up format).
4. **"Polymarket vs Kalshi vs Ledge"** — contrarian comparison; your hook = "one of these won't take your rent money."
5. **"The 5 types of Ledge bettor — which one are you?"** — personality/tag-a-friend bait (high shares).

**Operating cadence (lean start):**
- 1 carousel + 1 short video / day, faceless, app-screens only.
- Each post ends with the same CTA + un-croppable logo (screenshot marketing).
- Repost every user who "calls it."
- Speed > polish. Turn a fresh market into a post within the hour.
